Apple.com

Apple's website is known for its clean, minimalist design and focus on product showcasing.

  • Contrast: Apple uses strong contrast with white space and high-quality product images, making key elements stand out.
  • Repetition: Consistent fonts, button styles, and layout patterns are repeated throughout the site, reinforcing branding.
  • Alignment: Elements are precisely aligned, creating a sense of order and professionalism.
  • Proximity: Related information is grouped logically, making it easy for users to navigate and understand the content.

Craigslist.org

Craigslist is a classic example of a utilitarian website that prioritizes functionality over aesthetics. Its design is often considered outdated and cluttered.

  • Contrast: Craigslist uses minimal contrast, relying heavily on plain text and basic links, which can make it difficult to distinguish important elements.
  • Repetition: While Craigslist maintains a consistent layout across its various sections, the overall design lacks visual repetition that would enhance its aesthetic appeal.
  • Alignment: Elements on Craigslist are generally aligned, but the lack of visual hierarchy and consistent spacing contributes to a cluttered appearance.
  • Proximity: Related information is often grouped, but the sheer volume of text and links can overwhelm users and make it difficult to find specific items.
